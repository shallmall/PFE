Walkthrough: Late Fusion Score-Level Integration & Reputation AI
We built an isolated, production-grade Late Fusion Score-Level Integration pipeline inside late_Fusion_Score-Level_Integration/. This experiment combines NLP text analysis from our fine-tuned BERT model, structural topology anomaly scores from our temporal HGAT model, and point-in-time historical rolling features to train and benchmark four distinct machine learning architectures.

🏗️ Model Architecture Structure (Dual-Model Setup)
Because standard gradient boosting and linear algorithms are designed to predict one target at a time, our Late Fusion system operates as 2 separate models running side-by-side:

🛍️ Product Reputation Head (model_product):
Takes 6 point-in-time features.
Trained on 100% of transactions (381,734 rows) using supervised product fraud labels (fake_review_product).
👤 Reviewer Reputation Head (model_reviewer):
Takes the exact same 6 point-in-time features.
Trained only on the ~21% labeled reviewer rows using Semi-Supervised Masking. The 79% unlabeled rows are safely ignored during loss calculation while preserving continuous inference capabilities.
🛠️ What Was Built
1. Realistic Point-in-Time Temporal Feature Engine
Created 
prepare_temporal_features.py
 to merge 381,734 review records across BERT and HGAT outputs.

Applied strict chronological sorting by review_date.
Calculated 6 zero-leakage point-in-time features strictly prior to each review (shift(1)):
bert_spam_prob
gnn_reviewer_score
gnn_product_score
user_historical_fake_ratio
user_avg_past_bert_score
product_historical_fake_ratio (Tracks historical flagged suspicious reviews rather than ground truth to ensure realistic production evaluation)
2. Multi-Model Training & Benchmarking Pipeline
Created 
train_late_fusion_models.py
 to evaluate models on a chronological split (80% Train, 10% Val, 10% Test).
